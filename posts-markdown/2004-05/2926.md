It would appear as though we have made the news! [The Register.](http://www.theregister.co.uk) has an article on the research which was carried out recently by [Invest NI](http://www.investni.com) showing that Belfast [technology workers are some of the lowest paid in Europe.](http://www.theregister.co.uk/2004/05/12/ni_it_wages/)   
  
Only Bangalore in India, and Budapest and Prague in Eastern Europe, have lower salary levels.  
  
The data also shows that IT workers in Dublin are paid dramatically more than Belfast workers, with wages coming in 27 per cent higher on average in the capital of the Republic of Ireland.  
  
Possibly the most expensive city on Earth to hire IT staff is San Francisco, where a head of IT will earn some €165,331 per year, on average, compared to €91,251 in Belfast and €107,253 in Dublin.  
  
Is this fair!?
